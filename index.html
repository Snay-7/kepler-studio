The problem

Your website is a brochure. It should be a sales engine.

You're paying for traffic — Google Ads, Meta, SEO, referrals — and most of it lands on a page that asks visitors to scroll, read, find a phone number, and call you back during office hours. That's a leaky funnel by design.

The leads that do come through end up in three different inboxes, a notebook, and someone's WhatsApp. By the time you follow up, they've booked with the competitor next door.

Kepler rebuilds the front door (a website that actually captures leads) and the engine behind it (CRM, automations, AI agents) so nothing falls through the gaps.

Common questions, straight answers.

Don't see yours? Email hello@kepler.studio and we'll reply within a working day.

How long does a typical build take?
Six weeks for the Operate package, four for Launch, eight for Scale. We commit to the date in the proposal — and we hit it.
What does "captures real leads" actually mean?
Forms designed around buyer intent, progressive profiling so visitors aren't asked for ten fields up-front, and analytics that show you which pages are bringing in real money — not just traffic. Every lead that comes through is qualified before it hits a sales rep.
Do I own the website and the data?
Yes — both. The code lives in a Git repo we hand over to you. The data lives in a database you can export at any time. If you ever leave Kepler, you walk out with everything.
What if I want to leave?
Give us 30 days notice. We export every asset, hand over credentials, document the stack, and stay reachable for 60 days while your new team gets settled.
Can you integrate with what we already have?
Almost certainly. We work with Stripe, Calendly, HubSpot, Pipedrive, Mailchimp, Klaviyo, ActiveCampaign, Twilio, and most CMSs. If it has an API, we can plug into it.
Why not just hire someone in-house?
Because one person can't be a designer, a CRM admin, an automations engineer and an AI specialist. Kepler gives you that team — and the systems they've already built — for less than the cost of one in-house hire.
